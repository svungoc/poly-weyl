val moyal : t → t → t

moyal f g is the Weyl deformation of the Poisson bracket. It is equal to \(i/\hbar\) times the Moyal bracket of f and g (not the Moyal product):

\[ \frac{i}{\hbar}[ f,g ] (q,p , \hbar ) = \Delta^* \sum_{k\geq 0} (-1)^{k} \frac{\hbar^{2k}}{2^{2k} (2k+1)!} \Pi^{2k+1} (f\otimes g) = \{f,g\} + O(\hbar^3) , \]

where \(\Pi\) is the bi-Poisson operator and \(\Delta^*\) is the contraction operator (PolyTensor.contract). See also the wikipedia page, but the sign convention there is opposite.

We don't define the Moyal star product here, because it requires complex coefficients. See the CWeyl module.
